Dema Niha (Present Tense)

Kurdish has one present tense: Dema Niha (Present Tense). The same form is used for both habitual and ongoing actions - the difference is shown through context and time markers like "niha" (now) or "her roj" (every day).

Formation

Usage:

Used for both habitual and ongoing actions. The same form expresses:

  • Habitual actions: "Ez her roj diçim" (I go every day)
  • Ongoing actions: "Ez niha diçim" (I am going now)
  • General truths and facts

Formation:

di- + verb stem + personal ending

Time Markers to Show Context:

Time Marker Meaning Example
niha now (ongoing) Ez niha dixwim (I am eating now)
her roj every day (habitual) Ez her roj dixwim (I eat every day)
her gav always (habitual) Ez her gav diçim (I always go)
vê gavê right now (ongoing) Ez vê gavê dixwim (I am eating right now)
