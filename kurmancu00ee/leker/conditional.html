Dema Şertî (Conditional)

Kurdish has two main ways to express conditional: using conditional sentences with eger (if), or using a specific conditional form with the suffix -ma or -maye.

1. Conditional with "eger" (if)

Formation:

If-clause: eger + subjunctive form
Main clause: dê + subjunctive form (same as future)

Example:

Eger ez çim, tu jî dê bêyî
If I go, you will come too

2. Conditional with -ma / -maye

Formation:

Subjunctive form + -ma or -maye

Example:

Ez biçûma or Ez biçûmaye
I would go

Usage:

This form expresses a more definite conditional meaning ("would") and is often used without an if-clause.

Conditional Examples with 9 Verbs

1. bûn (to be)
Form 1: eger + subjunctive Form 2: -ma/-maye English
Eger ez bim Ez bûma / Ez bûmaye If I were / I would be
Example: Eger ez baş bim, dê bêm - If I am well, I will come
Form 2 Full Conjugation (-ma/-maye):
Pronoun Conjugation English
Ez bûma / bûmaye I would be
Tu bûya / bûyaye You would be
Ew bûya / bûyaye He/She would be
Em bûna / bûnaye We would be
Hûn bûna / bûnaye You would be
Ew bûna / bûnaye They would be
2. heye (to have)
Form 1: eger + subjunctive Form 2: -ma/-maye English
Eger min ... hebe ... hebûma / ... hebûmaye If I have / I would have
Example: Eger min pere hebe, dê biçim - If I have money, I will go
Form 2 Full Conjugation (-ma/-maye):
Pronoun Conjugation English
Min ... hebûma / hebûmaye I would have
Te ... hebûya / hebûyaye You would have
Wî/Wê ... hebûya / hebûyaye He/She would have
Me ... hebûna / hebûnaye We would have
We ... hebûna / hebûnaye You would have
Wan ... hebûna / hebûnaye They would have
